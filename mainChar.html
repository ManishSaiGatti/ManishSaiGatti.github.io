Main Character - Assassin's Creed Origins

Bayek of Siwa is the main protagonist of Assassin's Creed Origins. He is a Medjay, an elite group of warriors and protectors in ancient Egypt. Bayek hails from the small town of Siwa, located in the western desert region of Egypt.

A skilled warrior and hunter, Bayek is proficient in various weapons such as the iconic hidden blade, bows, swords, and spears. He is also a master of stealth and is adept at scaling walls and buildings, allowing him to navigate the vast open-world environments of ancient Egypt with ease.

Driven by a personal tragedy, Bayek embarks on a quest for vengeance that eventually leads him to uncover a greater conspiracy threatening the freedom of all Egyptians. Along the way, he forms an unlikely alliance with his wife, Aya, and together they lay the foundations for the Assassin Brotherhood, a secret organization dedicated to fighting against oppression and protecting the free will of mankind.

Bayek's character is shaped by his strong sense of justice and his unwavering commitment to protecting the innocents. He is a fierce warrior but also possesses a warm and compassionate side, particularly when it comes to his interactions with children and those in need. His deep connection to the ancient Egyptian culture and his reverence for the gods and traditions of his people are evident throughout the game.

With his rugged appearance, distinctive eagle-headed outfit, and memorable voice, Bayek has become an iconic figure in the Assassin's Creed series. His journey through ancient Egypt not only sets the stage for the birth of the Assassin Brotherhood but also offers players a immersive exploration of one of the most fascinating periods in human history.
